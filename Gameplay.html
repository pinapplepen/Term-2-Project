Gameplay

Introduction

Company of Heroes 2 provides an unique individual squad control experience that requires intensive micromanaging and stratagizing. It also has an interesting cover system which will provide the squad with buffs or debuffs depending on what cover they have. Most cover is directional so it promotes flanking and suprise attacks.

Territory Points

The entire game revloves around Territory Points. These are points that, if captured, will provide a steady stream of resources or buffs. There are 5 types of territory points in the game, Normal, Fuel, Munition, Fuel, Medical, and Victory. Most of the other points are self explanitory but Victory points are different. You can achieve victory by holding on to them for a certain amount of time. But remember, enemies can steal any point you caputre.

Commander Abilities

Many commanders have powerful abilities they can use in exchange for munitions. Some examples are artillery barrages, air strikes, incendiary bombs, smoke drops, etc. Some abilities are passive like giving a speed buff for a certain amount of time or temporarily hiding all friendly units on the enemy's mini map. Others unlock special new units/squad abilites.

Factions

There are 5 factions in the game. Soviet, Wehrmacht, USF (United States Forces), UKF (United Kingdom Forces), and OBK (Oberkommando). Soviet and Wehrmacht are in the base game while UKF, USF, and OBK are DLCs. Soviets have abysmal equipment but make up for it with their increased manpower gain. Wehrmacht and UKF are more defensive based and are able to build powerful buildings to hold areas of land. The OBK is an extreamly aggressive faction that excels in breaking enemy lines. USF is kind of in between the Soviets and Oberkommando. They have a slightly increased manpower rate but also have better weapons than the Soviets.
